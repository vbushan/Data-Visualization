Dataset¶

For this assignment, I have used the NYC Motor Vehicle Collisions and Crashes dataset. The dataset has more than 1.7 million rows. I downloaded the dataset from the https://data.cityofnewyork.us/Public-Safety/Motor-Vehicle-Collisions-Crashes/h9gi-nx95. The dataset is open to public and free for non-commerical use.

The dataset contains comprehensive information of motor vehicles collisions across NYC from 2012-2020. This dataset has been cited in a lot of research papers and news articles. My visualizations are inspired by a blog publised on nycdatascience.com. (Link- https://data.cityofnewyork.us/Public-Safety/Motor-Vehicle-Collisions-Crashes/h9gi-nx95). Code to generate the visualizations is solely my own. I used the article only as an inspiration to create these visualizations.

Note: Since the dataset is large, I was getting memory overflow issues- I had to save multiple copies of the data frame. So, I decided the split the plots into three notebooks.

Plot 1¶

The first plot illustrates the total collisions across various BOROUGHS in NYC over the years 2012-2020. I chose to use a grouped bar graph for this plot, since I am comparing the total collisions across BOROUGH each year.

Inference: From the plot, we can observe that over these 9 years, Brooklyn has been consistently most prone to Motor Vehicle Collisions.

Plot 2¶

The second plot illustrates a breakdown of the type of victims involved in the collisions. Again, I chose to use a grouped bar graph for this plot, since I am comparing the total between different types of victims each year.

Inference: Motorists are more prone to motor vehicle collisions. Although this conclusion seems redundant, on further analysis, I found out that it's actually the pedestrians who most "severly" affected by motor vehicle collisions.

Plot 3¶

The third plot is an illustartion of total number of victims across different times of a day. Since, I am trying to analyze the temporal distribution of total victims, I chose to use a line chart for this illustration.

Inference: From the plot, we can infer that most collisions have occured between 8AM and 4 PM across over these 8 years.
